Medic is [Scout](/blog/scout-the-storefront-auditor)'s internal twin. Scout audits
*public* storefronts and reports; Medic audits *owned* ones and then does the thing
Scout never does — it changes them. Medic is the fleet's healing agent, and the only
agent allowed to write to a live store. That privilege is exactly why its guardrails
are the most load-bearing part of its design.

Medic is a **scaffold** today, not an operational agent. The audit-and-propose half
is built; the half that actually mutates a live store is still open. This post is
the design — what Medic is meant to do and why it's shaped this way — with the parts
that don't run yet called out.

## The line between Scout and Medic

Both agents pull from the same `scan_requests` queue. One column decides which agent
gets a row: `is_internal`. Scout takes the public rows (`is_internal = false`); Medic
takes the owned ones (`is_internal = true`). Same queue, same audit engine, opposite
halves of the work.

| | Scout | Medic |
|---|---|---|
| Pulls | public rows (`is_internal = false`) | owned rows (`is_internal = true`) |
| Posture | polite visitor, read-only | stealth + proxy, read-write |
| Does | audit, then report | audit, then prioritize, experiment, promote or roll back, verify |
| Authorization | none needed — public | must be an owned or authorized host |

That `is_internal` flag is **fail-closed**, and it is Medic's Rule 1: re-assert the
flag on every row it claims, and refuse any row where `is_internal` isn't `true`. A
public row that somehow got mis-routed into Medic's lane can never receive a write —
the check is defense-in-depth, not a formality, precisely because the cost of getting
it wrong is a change to a store that isn't ours to change.

## Healing through experiments

The idea that shapes everything else: **Medic never just edits a store.** Every fix
ships as an **A/B experiment.** A proposed change becomes a *variant* running against
the current *control*, real traffic is split between them, and only a variant that
statistically wins gets promoted. A variant that loses rolls back. Nothing becomes
permanent on the strength of Medic's judgment alone — the traffic decides.

The phrase in Medic's own brief is worth keeping: *the experiment is the guardrail —
every change is measured and reversible by construction.* That is what makes it
acceptable to give one agent write access to a live store at all. It never bets the
store on being right; it bets a slice of traffic on a variant, and lets the numbers
overrule it.

## The guardrails that make write access survivable

Medic is the highest-privilege agent in the fleet, so its safety rails are not
optional add-ons — they are the reason it can exist:

- **The `is_internal` re-check (Rule 1).** Owned hosts only, re-verified per row,
  fail-closed.
- **The A/B harness.** No change is permanent until traffic votes for it; every
  change has a rollback path built in.
- **The apply-gate.** Medic runs in dry-run by default. It takes an explicit signal
  (`MEDIC_APPLY`) to leave dry-run and touch a live store — so "audit and propose"
  and "actually change" are two different, deliberately-separated modes.
- **Scoped credentials and a change audit log.** Writes go through scoped store
  credentials, and every change Medic makes is recorded (the `medic_changes` table),
  so there is always an answer to "what did it touch, and can we undo it."

## What's built, and what's still open

The **audit, prioritize, and propose** half exists: the `store-healer` plugin (the
experiment-recording MCP and the prioritize/experiment/decide skills) ships, and the
`medic_experiments`, `medic_experiment_metrics`, and `medic_changes` tables are
applied. Medic can audit an owned site, prioritize its findings, and record a
*proposed* experiment.

The **deploy** half — the part that lets a variant actually mutate a live store — is
open, pending sign-off: the A/B mechanism itself, the apply-gate wiring, the stealth
browser and residential-proxy egress, and the write providers (CAPTCHA solving,
Shopify admin writes). Until those land, Medic proposes and records; it does not
apply. When the deploy half is signed off and provisioned, this post gets the
operational detail — and Medic graduates from scaffold to a live entry in
[the fleet](/blog/the-agent-fleet).
