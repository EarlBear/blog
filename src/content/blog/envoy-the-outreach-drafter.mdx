*New here? [Start with the whole system](/blog/start-here-the-whole-system) — what EarlBear is and the stack this runs on. This post is one profile in [the agent fleet](/blog/the-agent-fleet).*

Envoy is the fleet's outreach drafter. It sits at the end of the public pipeline:
[Scout](/blog/scout-the-storefront-auditor) audits a public storefront and records
what got in the way of a sale, and Envoy turns that evidence into a *lead* — a
drafted, value-first pitch, filed into [Watson](/blog/watson-the-overnight-jira-loop)'s
Jira board for a human to review and send. Scout finds; Envoy pitches; Watson
carries. One direction, no loops back.

Envoy is a **scaffold** today, not an operational agent. The shape — read findings,
find a contact, draft a pitch, file a lead — is designed; the providers that would
let it discover a real contact and the compliance identity it would send under are
still open. This post is the design and the one rule it is built around.

## The one rule: drafts and queues, never sends

Everything about Envoy is shaped by a single guardrail, stated first-class in its
brief: **it drafts and queues, it never sends.** Envoy produces a pitch and files it
as a lead. A human reviews that lead and is the one who actually sends the message.

This is not caution for its own sake — it is the compliance boundary. Cold outreach
lives under CAN-SPAM, GDPR, and CASL, and the safe design is to keep an automated
agent on the *drafting* side of the line and a person on the *sending* side. Envoy
never touches a send path; the worst case for a bad draft is that a reviewer deletes
it, not that it reaches an inbox. Keeping "generate" and "send" as two different
actors, split by a human, is the rule the rest of the design serves.

## From finding to lead

Envoy's loop is a one-directional handoff. It reads Scout's findings **read-only** —
it never writes back to the scan tables — discovers the business contact behind the
store, drafts a pitch built around the specific friction Scout found, and files that
as a lead in Watson's pipeline.

The pitch is deliberately **value-first** — an "audit teardown" that leads with the
specific problem Scout found on that store, not a generic template blast. The lead
Envoy files carries that evidence, so the human reviewing it sees exactly why this
store, and exactly what to say.

## Where it plugs into the fleet

Envoy is the connective tissue between the two halves of the public pipeline. Scout's
whole job is to *see clearly and report* — it has no write access to Jira and cannot
turn a finding into an action. Watson works the Jira board but doesn't go looking for
new business. Envoy is the step in between: it reads what Scout saw and files
something Watson can carry, as an EARL Jira lead (`kind:email` or `kind:pitch-deck`)
that lands in the same pipeline Watson works overnight. Findings become leads become
board work — each agent doing only its own stage.

## What's built, and what's still open

The **shape** exists: the skills that define the loop — pull a qualified lead from the
findings, find the contact, draft the pitch, file the lead — are scaffolded, and the
Scout-to-Envoy-to-Watson handoff is designed one-directional and read-only against the
scan tables.

The pieces that would make Envoy *operational* are open, and they are exactly the ones
that carry real-world risk:

- **A contact-discovery provider** — the email/contact finder Envoy would use to learn
  who to reach. Not yet chosen or wired.
- **A compliance identity** — the sender identity and physical mailing address that
  lawful outreach requires (`ENVOY_FROM_*`, `ENVOY_PHYSICAL_ADDRESS`). Envoy can't
  draft under a real identity until these exist.
- **A funnel and suppression store** — an `outreach_leads` table to track a lead's
  state and honor do-not-contact, so the same business isn't pitched twice and opt-outs
  are respected.

No secrets are provisioned for Envoy yet. Until the contact-discovery and compliance
pieces land, it stays a scaffold — and even when they do, the never-send rule doesn't
move: a human stays on the send side. When Envoy comes online, this post gets the
operational detail and it graduates to a live entry in
[the fleet](/blog/the-agent-fleet).
